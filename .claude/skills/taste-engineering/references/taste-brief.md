# Project Taste Brief — NECYPAA XXXVI Website

> Generated from project profile. Edit directly or regenerate with /taste-brief.

## Project Identity

### This project believes:
Recovery is not sterile. A convention website for young people in AA should
feel like the energy of the rooms — alive, welcoming, a little chaotic in the
best way — not like a hospital intake form or a corporate conference portal.
Accessibility is non-negotiable (WCAG AAA) but accessibility and personality
are not in tension. The Digital Common Welfare Standard proves that.

### This project is for:
Young people (18-35) in recovery or recovery-curious across 13 New England
states. They're skeptical of institutions, fluent in internet culture, used to
beautiful digital experiences, and desperately need to feel like they belong
somewhere. They've seen enough clinical recovery branding to last a lifetime.

### This project should feel like:
- A Patagonia catalog: earnest without being preachy, beautiful without
  trying too hard, text-dense but every word earned
- A Criterion Collection film page: curated, intentional, treats its
  audience as intelligent
- The lobby of a really good coffee shop in a recovery-heavy neighborhood:
  warm lighting, real materials, zero corporate energy

### This project should NEVER feel like:
- A hospital or treatment center website (sterile, clinical, stock photos
  of sunrise silhouettes)
- A tech startup landing page (hero + three features + pricing)
- A generic event management platform (Eventbrite default template energy)
- Anything that signals "we couldn't afford a real designer" OR "we're
  trying too hard to be cool"

### The one word that MUST describe this project: ALIVE
### The one word that must NEVER describe this project: CLINICAL

## Anti-References (projects whose DNA we reject):
- Generic AA intergroup websites with 1998 HTML energy
- Treatment center websites with stock photography of held hands
- Conference sites that are just an Eventbrite embed with a logo

## Unexpected References (outside this space):
- Patagonia catalog: earnest, beautiful, text-dense, every word earned
- Criterion Collection film pages: curated, intentional, intelligent audience
- A good coffee shop lobby in a recovery neighborhood: warm, real, zero corporate

## Taste Constraints (rules we won't break):
- Bilingual (EN/ES) is not an afterthought — it's first-class throughout
- Every image must be real (from actual NECYPAA events) or intentionally
  illustrated. Zero stock photography.
- Typography must have warmth. No cold geometric sans-serifs.
- The site must feel handmade even though it's built with modern tooling.
  Handmade means: deliberate imperfections, asymmetric layouts where
  appropriate, content-driven structure not template-driven.
