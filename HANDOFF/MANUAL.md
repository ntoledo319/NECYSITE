# Part I — Before You Touch Anything

## 1. What this website actually does

necypaact.com is the convention website for the **Northeast Convention of Young People in Alcoholics Anonymous, 36th edition** (NECYPAA XXXVI). The convention happens once a year. This site is up year-round.

The site does four jobs:

1. **Tells people what NECYPAA is and when it happens.** Homepage, FAQ, schedule, hotel info.
2. **Sells registration tickets.** When someone clicks "Register," they fill out a form and pay a real credit card. Real dollars land in a real bank account controlled by the convention committee.
3. **Sells optional add-ons.** Breakfast tickets, merchandise. Same payment flow.
4. **Gives scholarships and access codes.** Some people register without paying. The site supports comp codes generated by the committee.

That's the whole job. Everything in this manual is in service of one of those four things.

## 2. The four laws (these override everything)

These come from outside this codebase. No technical concern overrides them.

### Law 1 — Anonymity is preserved on the public site

**Rule:** No full last names of any AA member on the public site. First name + last initial only ("Sarah K.", not "Sarah Kowalski"). No identifiable faces of AA members in photos.

**Why:** Tradition Eleven of Alcoholics Anonymous. Public anonymity is non-negotiable. People's recoveries, jobs, and family relationships depend on it.

**When this comes up:** Adding a speaker name. Posting a photo from last year's convention. Writing a blog post that mentions a real person. Quoting somebody.

**What to do if your AI proposes a violation:** Stop. Tell it: *"Use first name + last initial only. AA traditions override this. Re-read HANDOFF/MANUAL.md section 2."* Then make the change yourself.

### Law 2 — Attraction, not promotion

**Rule:** The site shares facts about the convention. It does not "sell" recovery, endorse AA, or make claims about what AA can do for someone.

**Why:** Tradition Eleven again. AA is for people who want it. The site provides information for those who are looking; it does not push.

**Tell-tales of a promotional violation:** Marketing-speak ("transform your life," "discover the power of recovery"), urgency tactics ("register now or miss out forever"), testimonials phrased as endorsements ("AA cured me"), comparisons to other recovery programs.

**Tell-tales of attraction (correct):** Plain facts. Dates, times, what happens at the convention. Personal stories told in first person without claiming AA "fixed" anything. Soft invitations, not sales pitches.

### Law 3 — No money handling outside Stripe

**Rule:** All payments flow through Stripe. The site does not collect PayPal, Venmo, Zelle, gift cards, or cash. Refunds happen in Stripe. Disputes happen in Stripe.

**Why:** Stripe is PCI-compliant. Anything else exposes the committee to legal and financial risk.

**When this comes up:** Someone emails asking if they can pay you via Venmo. Answer: politely, no, only through the registration page.

### Law 4 — The site never claims to speak for AA as a whole

**Rule:** Don't add language like "AA officially supports..." or "AA recommends...". The convention is hosted by a NECYPAA committee. The committee is independent. The website speaks for the convention only.

**Why:** Tradition Six. No AA group ever endorses, finances, or lends the AA name to any related facility or outside enterprise.

## 3. Your relationship with your AI

Your AI assistant (Claude, Codex, ChatGPT, whatever you're paying $20/month for) is your hands. You are the brain. This relationship works only if you treat it correctly.

### What your AI is good at

- Reading existing code and explaining what it does.
- Making small edits when you describe them precisely.
- Catching obvious typos and bugs in code it wrote.
- Following step-by-step instructions in this manual.

### What your AI is **bad** at, and where you must override it

- **It is bad at saying "I don't know."** When it isn't sure, it guesses confidently. If your AI tells you something will work and you're not sure, ask: *"Walk me through how you know that will work. What would you check first?"*
- **It is bad at understanding context outside the code.** It doesn't know that today is the day before registration opens. It doesn't know the committee chair's preferences. You have to tell it.
- **It is bad at saying "this is risky, let's stop."** You have to be the one who pumps the brakes when something feels wrong.
- **It is bad at the AA Traditions.** It will sometimes suggest marketing copy or use full names. You catch that.

### The rules for talking to your AI

1. **Always tell it you have no coding experience.** Every new conversation. Otherwise it talks to you like a peer engineer and you'll miss things.
2. **Always tell it the project folder path.** It's `/Users/nicholastoledo/Development/A.A/NECYPASITE` — update this if the folder ever moves.
3. **Always ask it to plan before it acts.** "Show me what you're going to do. Don't do it yet."
4. **Always ask it to open a pull request, never to push directly to main.** A pull request is a change that's waiting for your approval. It's reversible. A direct push to main is live immediately.
5. **Always ask it to run `pnpm build` after a change** and to stop if the build fails. The build is the proof the change didn't break anything obvious.
6. **Always ask it to update the HANDOFF folder** if it touched something documented here.

The prompt templates in Part III bake all of this in. Use them.

## 4. The Two-Windows rule

When you make any change to the live site, keep **two browser windows open** side by side:

- **Left window:** the live site at https://www.necypaact.com — what users see.
- **Right window:** Vercel at https://vercel.com → project `necypaa-ct` → Deployments tab.

After every change:
1. Watch Vercel for the new deployment to finish (green checkmark).
2. Refresh the left window. Look at the change with your own eyes.
3. If it looks wrong, hit the rollback button (see QUICKSTART section 3).

This rule has prevented more disasters than any code review.

## 6. GitHub

**What it is:** The storage site where all the code lives. Think of it as Google Drive for code. The repo URL is: https://github.com/ntoledo319/NECYPASITE

**What you actually do on GitHub:**

- **Review pull requests.** When your AI creates a "pull request," it shows up on GitHub at the **Pull Requests** tab. You open it, look at the change (highlighted green for added lines, red for removed), and either click **Merge pull request** (approve) or close it (reject). Your AI should always create one of these for any change.
- **Look at history.** The **Commits** tab is a list of every change ever made. Helpful for "when did X get changed?"
- **Nothing else.** Don't click the red "Delete repository" buttons. Don't accept invitations from people you don't recognize. Don't change settings.

**You will rarely log into GitHub directly.** Your AI talks to GitHub for you. You only show up to approve pull requests.

## 7. Vercel (where the website lives)

**What it is:** The hosting service that turns the code in GitHub into the live website at necypaact.com. When code changes in GitHub, Vercel automatically rebuilds the site within about 2 minutes.

**Login:** https://vercel.com → log in (see [`ACCOUNTS.md`](./ACCOUNTS.md) for which login to use).

**What you do here:**

- **Watch deployments finish** (the Deployments tab). Green checkmark = good. Red X = the change broke something.
- **Roll back when things go wrong** (QUICKSTART section 3).
- **Look at runtime logs** if something is mysteriously broken. Logs tab. Your AI can read these for you.
- **Manage environment variables** (passwords/keys the site needs). You almost never touch these. If you do, see Section 29.

**Cost:** Vercel charges based on traffic. Currently the site is on the Hobby (free) plan or Pro (paid). If you ever get a "you've exceeded your plan" email, don't ignore it — see [`ACCOUNTS.md`](./ACCOUNTS.md).

## 8. Stripe (where the money lives)

**What it is:** The payment processor. When someone pays for registration, the money goes from their card → Stripe → after Stripe takes a small fee, into the convention bank account (whoever the committee linked).

**Login:** https://dashboard.stripe.com → log in (see [`ACCOUNTS.md`](./ACCOUNTS.md)).

**What you do here:**

- **Look at payments** (the Payments tab). Each row is one person who registered.
- **Issue refunds.** Click a payment → click **Refund**. See Section 17.
- **Respond to disputes** (Section 28). These come with a real legal deadline. Don't ignore them.
- **Watch the balance.** The **Balance** tab shows how much money is sitting in Stripe waiting to be paid out to the bank. Payouts happen automatically.

**Two Stripe modes:**

- **Test mode** — fake money, for development. Toggle in the top right of the dashboard.
- **Live mode** — real money. **Always confirm you're in Live mode before issuing a refund.**

**Do not:**

- Rotate the API keys without expert help (Section 29).
- Add new "Products" through the Stripe UI — the site's products are auto-created by code. Manual ones will conflict.
- Disconnect the linked bank account without a written succession plan.

**Login:** see [`ACCOUNTS.md`](./ACCOUNTS.md) — the admin user is set up there. If no admin user exists, the first person to register at `/admin` becomes the admin, so do not share that URL publicly.

## 10. Google (calendar + email)

If the Google Calendar doesn't update on the site, ask your AI to check the `GOOGLE_CALENDAR_API_KEY` environment variable in Vercel.

Every recipe below has the same five beats:

4. **How to undo it** (the rollback path).
5. **When to escalate** instead of doing it.

The boilerplate at the top of every prompt is the same. To save space, when a prompt says **`[STANDARD HEADER]`** copy this in:

```
I am the operator of necypaact.com. I have zero coding experience.

Read HANDOFF/AI_BRIEFING.md before doing anything else.
Default behaviors I expect from you:
  - Plan first, show me the plan, wait for me to say "go".
  - Always open a pull request, never push directly to main.
  - Run `pnpm build` after every change. Stop if it fails.
  - If the change touches anything documented in HANDOFF/, run /handoff-sync.

```

## 11. Change text on a page

**What you're doing:** Editing a heading, paragraph, button label, or any visible text on a page.

**Two cases:** if the text is in **English on the live site**, it lives in the i18n files (`messages/en.json`) for most pages, or directly inside a page file. If it's in **Spanish**, see Section 24.

**The prompt:**

```
[STANDARD HEADER]

I want to change this text:
  OLD: [paste the EXACT text — copy it from the live site]
  NEW: [paste the new text]

It appears on this page: [paste the URL]

Plan first:
1. Find where the OLD text lives — check messages/en.json first, then the page file.
2. Tell me the file path and line number.
3. Confirm the change does not violate AA Traditions
   (no full names, no promotional language).
4. After I say "go", make the change, run `pnpm build`, open a PR.
```

**How to verify:**

1. Wait for the PR to be merged and Vercel to deploy (Two-Windows rule).
2. Open the page in a private/incognito browser window (so you bypass cache).
3. Read the text. Confirm it changed.

**How to undo:**

- If the PR is still open: close it on GitHub. Nothing changed live.
- If it's already merged: roll back via Vercel (QUICKSTART section 3) and ask your AI to revert the commit.

**When to escalate:**

- The text is on the home page hero — that's the front door of the site and design-tuned. Get a second opinion.
- The change is a legal disclaimer, a refund policy, or anything that affects what people are agreeing to when they register.

## 12. Change a price

**What you're doing:** Changing the dollar amount someone pays for registration or breakfast tickets.

### The easy way (no code, no AI)

Almost all price changes go through the admin panel — no code change needed.

1. Go to https://www.necypaact.com/admin → log in.
2. **Globals → Pricing Settings**.
3. Update the price. **Prices are in cents.** $250 = `25000`. $35 = `3500`.
4. Click **Save**.
5. Open https://www.necypaact.com/register in an incognito window. Confirm the new price displays.

That's it. The site reads live prices from the database on every checkout. Past payments are unaffected.

### The hard way (when the admin panel is broken, or you want to change the default fallback)

The codebase has compiled-in defaults that act as a safety net if the admin panel database is unreachable. Those defaults live in `lib/registration-products.ts`. Changing them is a code change.

**The prompt:**

```
[STANDARD HEADER]

I want to change the fallback default price for [REGISTRATION / FRIDAY BREAKFAST / SATURDAY BREAKFAST / SUNDAY BREAKFAST]
from $[OLD] to $[NEW].

Important context:
- The live price comes from Payload (CMS database). The constant is a fallback.
- I have ALREADY updated the live price in the admin panel (or I have not — tell me which).
- Past payments must not be affected.

Plan first:
1. Find the constant in lib/registration-products.ts.
2. Show me the line you'd change.
3. After I say "go", change it, run `pnpm build`, open a PR titled
   "fix(pricing): update [PRODUCT] fallback to $[NEW]".
4. Run /handoff-sync since this affects documented pricing.
```

**How to verify:** Same as Section 12-easy. Incognito window, /register page, look at the price.

**How to undo:** Admin panel: just change the price back. Code change: roll back via Vercel + revert the PR.

**When to escalate:**

- You're cutting a price mid-event (people who paid full price will be angry — talk to the committee chair first).
- You're raising a price for a tier that's already open for registration (you need a clear cutoff communication plan).

## 13. Change the convention dates or venue

**What you're doing:** Updating the convention's official dates or hotel location.

> ⚠️ **High-impact change.** Dates and venue appear in 30+ places: page metadata, search-engine results, social-share cards, transactional emails, the FAQ, the schedule, the hotel-booking link. A half-finished change can leave the site contradicting itself. **Do not do this in a hurry.**

**The prompt:**

```
[STANDARD HEADER]

I need to change the convention dates and/or venue.

  Current dates: Dec 31, 2026 – Jan 3, 2027
  Current venue: Hartford Marriott Downtown, Hartford, CT
  New dates: [PASTE]
  New venue: [PASTE]
  New hotel booking URL (if changed): [PASTE]
  New convention start ISO timestamp (for the countdown): [PASTE, like 2027-12-31T18:00:00-05:00]

This is a high-impact change. Plan exhaustively first:
1. Find every reference to the dates and venue in the codebase.
   Start with lib/constants.ts but check every file.
2. Find every reference in messages/en.json AND messages/es.json.
3. Find every reference in collections/ that might cache the values.
4. Show me the FULL LIST of files you'd change. Do not change anything yet.
5. After I say "go", make all changes in ONE pull request.
6. Run `pnpm build`. If it fails, STOP and tell me.
7. After the build passes, list any place a human still needs to update manually
   (e.g. the hotel deal expiration date, blog posts that reference the date, etc.).
8. Run /handoff-sync — this changes documented values.
```

**How to verify:**

1. Open the homepage. Date shown correctly.
2. Open /faq. Date and venue correct.
3. Open /register. Date and venue correct in the page header.
4. Search the live site for the old date with `site:necypaact.com [OLD DATE]` on Google. Some pages may take 1–2 weeks to re-index, but if you find live mentions of the old date on the site itself, those got missed.
5. Send yourself a test registration (use a Stripe test card if in test mode) and check that the confirmation email says the new date.

**How to undo:** Roll back via Vercel.

**When to escalate:**

- Any change like this should be coordinated with whoever maintains social media, printed flyers, the bid packet PDF, and the AA central office that publishes the convention listings. The website is one of many places the date lives.

## 17. Issue a refund

**What you're doing:** Returning someone's registration payment.

1. https://dashboard.stripe.com → log in → confirm you are in **Live mode** (top right).
2. Click **Payments** in the sidebar.
3. Find the payment — search by the registrant's email or last 4 of their card.
4. Click the payment row.
5. Click the **`...`** menu in the top right → **Refund payment**.
6. Choose **Full refund** or **Partial** with amount.
7. Add a brief internal note: who requested it, why, who approved it.
8. Click **Refund**.

The registrant gets the money back in 5–10 business days. Their registration on the site is **not automatically removed** — you have to also go into the admin panel → Registrations → find their row → mark it as refunded (there is a status field). If your AI confirms that the admin Registrations collection has a refund-status workflow, follow it; otherwise add a note.

**How to verify:** Stripe shows a green "Refunded" badge next to the payment. The registrant gets an automatic email from Stripe.

**How to undo:** Stripe refunds **cannot be cancelled** once issued. The money is on its way back. If you refunded by mistake, the registrant has to re-pay through the site.

**When to escalate:**

- Refunds over $500.
- Refunds within 48 hours of the convention (timing matters — they may already be at the venue).
- Any refund a registrant is angry about. Get the committee chair involved.

## 18. Issue a scholarship or comp registration code

**What you're doing:** Creating a code someone can enter at checkout to register for free.

1. Admin panel → **GiftCodes** → **Create new**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Code** — pick something memorable but not guessable. Convention initials + a word + a number. ("XXXVI-LOVE-2026").
   - **Type** — scholarship vs. comp vs. group. Use scholarship for individual hardship grants, comp for committee/speaker comps.
   - **Quantity** — usually 1.
   - **Expiration** — set it for the day registration closes.
   - **Notes** — who it's for and why (internal only; the recipient doesn't see this).
3. Save.
4. Email the code to the recipient with a link to https://www.necypaact.com/register and instructions on where to enter it.

**How to verify:** Open /register in incognito, start the form, find the "I have a code" field, paste the code. Confirm the price drops to $0 (or whatever the discount is).

**How to undo:** Admin panel → GiftCodes → find the code → **Void / Expire**. Already-used codes can't be undone.

**When to escalate:**

- Issuing more than 5 scholarships at once (check with the committee chair on the scholarship-fund balance).

## 19. Reply to "I paid but I'm not on the list"

99% of the time, this is one of:

- **The payment is still pending in Stripe.** Check `dashboard.stripe.com` → Payments. If status is "Processing," wait an hour and check again.
- **They paid as a different name than they registered.** Search Stripe by email instead of name.
- **Their email got auto-filed in spam.** Ask them to check spam/promotions for an email from `info@necypaa.org` or from Stripe.

**The reply template:**

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for getting in touch. I just looked up your payment in our system —
[I see it / I don't see it yet].

[If you see it]
You're confirmed for NECYPAA XXXVI. The confirmation email may have landed
in your spam or promotions folder; check there for a message from
info@necypaa.org. I'm also forwarding the confirmation now.

[If you don't see it]
Could you send me the date and last 4 digits of the card you used? I'll
track it down. If the payment hasn't gone through yet, no charge has been
made and you can try the registration again.

— [Your name]
```

**If you cannot find the payment in Stripe after 24 hours and they have a credit-card statement showing the charge:** something is genuinely wrong. Escalate. Do not just refund — that creates a duplicate.

## 20. Reply to "I want my money back"

**Default policy:** Refunds are issued in full up to [POLICY DATE — fill in from the registration page]. After that date, refunds are case-by-case at committee discretion.

**Read the registration page's refund policy aloud** before replying. It is the contract you have with them.

**Then:**

1. Look up the payment in Stripe.
2. If they're inside the refund window, refund (Section 17).
3. If outside the window, reply with empathy and explain the policy. If the case is compelling (medical, family emergency), bring it to the committee.

## 21. Take registration down temporarily

**What you're doing:** Stopping new registrations from coming in, without breaking the rest of the site.

```
[STANDARD HEADER]

I need to temporarily disable the /register page while keeping the rest of the
site live. Reasons may be: bug, capacity full, awaiting a fix.

Plan first:
1. The cleanest way is to add a `MAINTENANCE_MODE` env var in Vercel and have
   the register page show a "registration is currently closed" message
   when it's set. Confirm whether that exists in the code already.
2. If not, propose the smallest possible change to make /register show a
   "closed" message without affecting any other page. Show me the change.
3. After I say "go", open a PR. Title: "feat(register): maintenance switch".
```

**How to verify:** Open /register in incognito. You see the "closed" message. Open / (homepage). Everything else looks normal.

**How to undo:** Set the env var to off in Vercel → Settings → Environment Variables. Redeploy.

**When to escalate:**

- If your reason is "we're sold out" — confirm with the committee chair you've actually hit capacity before disabling.
- If your reason is a payment problem — check with Stripe support, the issue may be on their side and they need to be the ones to flip the switch.

## 22. Put registration back up

Reverse of Section 21. Flip the maintenance env var off in Vercel. Verify in incognito.

# Part IV — When Things Go Wrong

## 25. "The site is down"

Follow QUICKSTART section 1. If after the rollback the site is still broken, ask your AI:

```
[STANDARD HEADER]

The live site at necypaact.com is showing [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU SEE — error message,
blank page, etc.]. I have already tried rolling back the most recent
deployment on Vercel and it did not help.

Please:
1. Check Vercel's runtime logs at https://vercel.com (you may need me to give
   you a screenshot — ask).
2. Check whether Stripe is reporting an outage at https://status.stripe.com.
3. Check whether the SQLite database file (payload.db) is the issue.
4. Do NOT make any code changes yet. Tell me what you found and what you
   would change.
```

## 26. "My AI made a change and now the site looks broken"

1. Roll back via Vercel (QUICKSTART section 3).
2. Open the pull request your AI created on GitHub.
3. Tell your AI: *"The deploy from PR #[number] broke the live site. I have rolled back. Look at the diff in that PR and tell me which line is the likely cause. Do not make any fix until I approve a plan."*
4. Read the explanation. Decide whether to retry or abandon.

## 27. "Vercel sent me an email about a failed build"

A failed build means the latest code change has a syntax error or type error and Vercel **did not** deploy it. **The live site is fine** — Vercel only swaps to a new version after a successful build.

1. Don't panic. The live site is unaffected.
2. Ask your AI: *"The latest Vercel build failed. Pull the build log from [PASTE THE FAILED DEPLOYMENT URL] and explain what failed. Then propose a fix as a new PR. Do not push to the existing PR — open a new one."*
3. Approve / reject as normal.

## 28. "Stripe sent me an email about a dispute or chargeback"

A dispute means a registrant has told their credit-card company "this charge is wrong" and the bank is asking Stripe (and you) to prove the transaction was legitimate.

**This has a deadline.** Usually 7–14 days. Do not ignore it.

1. Open the Stripe email. Click the link to the dispute.
2. Read what the cardholder said.
3. Gather evidence: the registration record in the admin panel (their name, email, what they paid for, when), any email correspondence with them.
4. Either:
   - **Accept the dispute** if the charge was wrong. Stripe takes the money back. You lose the money plus the dispute fee. Easier; sometimes the right call.
   - **Submit evidence** if the charge was correct. Stripe forwards it to the bank. You have to wait 60–90 days for a verdict.
5. Always log the outcome in a note for committee records.

**Escalate to the committee chair** for any dispute over $250 or any pattern (multiple disputes from the same source).

## 29. "I get warnings about expiring keys / secrets"

The site has several "secret" values stored in Vercel's Environment Variables panel. They authenticate the site to other services (Stripe, the database, the rate-limit cache). Most never expire. A few might.

**If you get an expiration warning:**

1. Do not act yet. Open [`ACCOUNTS.md`](./ACCOUNTS.md) and identify the service.
2. Ask your AI: *"I got an expiration warning for [SERVICE]. Walk me through how to rotate this key safely. Plan first; do not change anything in Vercel until I approve."*
3. Rotation always means: generate the new key at the service, add it to Vercel as a new value, deploy, then revoke the old key. Out of order = downtime.

**You do not have to rotate keys on a schedule for safety.** Only rotate when:
- The provider says it's expiring.
- A key was leaked (committed accidentally, posted in a chat, emailed unencrypted).
- A team member with access leaves.

## B. Danger zones — never let your AI touch these without escalation

If an AI starts modifying any of the files / paths below, **stop the session** and contact the escalation contact in [`ACCOUNTS.md`](./ACCOUNTS.md) before continuing.

- `app/api/webhooks/stripe/` — the code that receives Stripe payment confirmations. A bug here means people pay and their registration silently disappears.
- `actions/registration.ts` — the main checkout server action. A bug here can either double-charge people or fail silently.
- `lib/env.ts` — the environment variable schema. A bug here can make Vercel refuse to start the site.
- `collections/Registrations.ts`, `collections/Donations.ts`, `collections/GroupRegistrations.ts` — the database shapes for paid records. Changing these mid-season can drop columns of customer data.
- Any change that **adds, removes, or renames** an environment variable. (Adding values to Vercel is one of the easiest things to get wrong.)
- Any change that touches Stripe API keys, webhook secrets, the bank account connection, or Stripe Connect status.
- Any change that modifies `.env`, `.env.local`, `.env.example` such that you no longer know what's in them.
- Any one-line change described by your AI as "small" but affecting more than 10 files. That's not a small change.
- Any change pushed **directly to main** rather than through a pull request.

## C. Glossary (every confusing word, defined)

| Word | What it means here |
| --- | --- |
| **Branch** | A side copy of the code where changes happen, until they're merged. "Pull request" is a request to merge a branch into main. |
| **Build** | The step where the code is compiled into the version that runs on the server. `pnpm build` runs it. A "failing build" means the change is broken and won't deploy. |
| **CMS** | Content Management System. The admin panel at /admin. WordPress is the famous example; we use Payload. |
| **Commit** | A saved snapshot of code changes, with a description. Like a save file. |
| **Deployment** | A specific live version of the site. Each successful build creates a deployment. Vercel keeps every one — that's how rollback works. |
| **Environment variable** (env var) | A password / configuration value the site needs but doesn't put in the public code. Stored in Vercel → Settings → Environment Variables. |
| **i18n** | "internationalization" — making the site work in multiple languages. Our i18n is English + Spanish. |
| **Main** | The official version of the code. What's running on the live site. |
| **Merge** | Combining a pull-request branch into main. Once merged, the change is on track to go live. |
| **Pull request** (PR) | A proposed code change waiting for your approval. Lives at github.com/ntoledo319/NECYPASITE/pulls. |
| **Push** | Sending code from a local computer up to GitHub. |
| **Repo / repository** | The GitHub project. Ours: github.com/ntoledo319/NECYPASITE. |
| **Rollback** | Reverting the live site to an earlier deployment. The Vercel button. |
| **Secret** | A password-like value (Stripe key, Payload secret). Lives in env vars; never in the code. |
| **Slug** | The URL-friendly part of a page address. `/blog/my-first-convention` → slug is `my-first-convention`. |
| **Stripe** | The payment processor. dashboard.stripe.com. |
| **Vercel** | The hosting provider. vercel.com. |
| **Webhook** | An automatic message Stripe sends our site whenever something happens (a payment, a refund). Our handler at `app/api/webhooks/stripe/` listens for them. |
