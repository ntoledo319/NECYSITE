> **Purpose:** Carry accurate A.A.-related information online while protecting anonymity, avoiding implied endorsement/affiliation, and staying aligned with A.A.'s Traditions and service guidance.
>
> **This file is LOCAL POLICY.** It is not "official A.A. rules." It reflects how _we_ choose to operate, informed by shared experience compiled in A.A. Guidelines and service material. In keeping with A.A.'s Tradition of autonomy (Tradition Four), most decisions are made by the group conscience of the members involved. These guardrails exist to assist in reaching an informed group conscience.
>
> **Scope:** This document governs both human contributors and AI agents (LLMs, chatbots, automated pipelines) that generate, edit, or publish content on behalf of this project.

## 0 · Non-Negotiables

### 0.1 The Internet Is Public Media

Anything publicly accessible on the internet — webpages, SEO snippets, images, video, podcasts, audio files, public social media posts, blog posts, and public app content — should be treated at the level of press, radio, and films.

**Source basis:** In 2013, the 63rd General Service Conference affirmed that the internet, social media, and all forms of public communications are implicit in the last phrase of the short form of Tradition Eleven, which reads: "…at the level of press, radio, and films."
_(MG-18, Rev. 10/25; SMF-197)_

When we post, text, or blog, we should assume that we are publishing at the public level. When we break our anonymity in these forums, we may inadvertently break the anonymity of others.
_(MG-18, Rev. 10/25; SMF-197)_

### 0.2 Our Default Stance

- **Attraction, not promotion.** We provide clear information so people can find help; we do not market, hype, or compete. _(Tradition 11)_
- **Principles before personalities.** We do not put people on display. _(Tradition 12)_
- **Group conscience governs.** Website committees and content decisions should flow from an informed group conscience, not unilateral action. _(Tradition 2; SMF-101 Q1)_
- **No need to rush.** There is no need to let the speed of technology dictate the speed of our actions. _(MG-18; SMF-101 Q1)_

## 3 · HARD RULES — Human Contributors

### 3.1 Anonymity at the Public Level

**Do NOT publish on public pages:**

- Full last names of A.A. members.
- Full-face photos or videos of A.A. members presented as A.A. members.
- Identifying personal stories that point to a specific, identifiable person.
- Screenshots of meetings, chats, rosters, or group emails with names, faces, or email addresses.
- Audio recordings of A.A. speakers where the speaker has not given consent for public distribution, or where full names or identifying details are disclosed.

**Operational notes:**

- If an image exists, assume it leaks something until proven otherwise.
- Even "closed" or "private" social media groups can reveal an individual's identity through group membership lists, tagged posts, or data breaches. Being well-informed prior to joining or starting such a group is key to protecting your own anonymity and that of others. _(MG-18, Rev. 10/25)_
- Password-protected sections for members only may contain additional member info (e.g., first name + last initial, service role contact info), but publicly accessible pages require the same safeguards as press, radio, and film. _(SMF-101 Q8; MG-18)_

**Self-check questions for social media** (adapted from MG-18, Rev. 10/25):

1. Is my posting about the message or the messenger?
2. Am I carrying the A.A. message in the hopes of helping someone else, or simply "shouting from the rooftops"?
3. Do my other posts and activities online create the appearance of A.A. affiliation or endorsement with outside issues?
4. Am I being extra cautious about not identifying other members as A.A. members in photographs or statements?
5. Am I avoiding the temptation to incite controversy?
6. Am I providing helpful links to my local A.A. website, aa.org, aagrapevine.org, or aalavina.org?

**Source basis:** Tradition 11 (short + long form); Tradition 12; MG-18 Rev. 10/25; SMF-197; SMF-101 Q8; 2013 GSC Advisory Action

### 3.2 Email Safety

- When sending A.A. email to multiple recipients who wish to remain anonymous, use BCC (Blind Courtesy Copy) or a mailing-list service that hides individual addresses. _(MG-18)_
- Obtain a recipient's explicit permission before using their email address for A.A. correspondence, especially if it is a workplace email address. _(MG-18)_
- Some committees maintain two versions of minutes: one for A.A. members only (with full names and contact info) and one for broader distribution (with identifying details removed). _(MG-18)_

**Source basis:** MG-18 Rev. 10/25; SMF-197

### 3.3 Domain Names and Trademarks

- Domain names should be decided by informed group conscience. _(SMF-101 Q3)_
- To preserve A.A.'s trademarks and service marks, avoid using the registered marks ("A.A.," "Alcoholics Anonymous," "The Big Book") in domain names. Shared experience shows many service entities integrate lowercase "aa" with other identifying information (e.g., `aacentraloffice.org`, `area999aa.org`). _(SMF-101 Q3)_
- When A.A. marks are used in text, they should be acknowledged as registered trademarks where appropriate (e.g., in footers or legal notices).

**Sample trademark acknowledgment for footers:**

> Alcoholics Anonymous®, A.A.®, and The Big Book® are registered trademarks of Alcoholics Anonymous World Services, Inc. The Grapevine®, A.A. Grapevine®, GV®, and La Viña® are registered trademarks of A.A. Grapevine, Inc.

**Source basis:** SMF-101 Q3; aa.org Terms of Use — Intellectual Property Policies

### 3.4 Hosting, Ads, and Self-Support

- In keeping with the Seventh Tradition, A.A. pays its own expenses — this applies in cyberspace too. _(MG-18; SMF-101 Q5)_
- Avoid hosts/services that require mandatory advertising space or links to commercial sites. Most A.A. website committees see mandatory ads as actual or implied affiliation or endorsement. _(MG-18; SMF-101 Q5)_
- Many "free" hosting services require mandatory ads or links. It is prudent to use a service that does not include mandatory advertisements. _(MG-18)_

**Source basis:** Tradition 7; MG-18 Rev. 10/25; SMF-101 Q5

### 3.5 Linking

**General principle:** Linking can broaden scope, but proceed with caution. _(MG-18; SMF-101 Q6)_

**Links to other A.A. sites:**

- Each A.A. entity is autonomous and has its own group conscience. A linked site may start displaying content your group conscience finds objectionable, and there is no way to know when this might occur. _(SMF-101 Q6)_

**Links to non-A.A. sites:**

- Even more problematic. Non-A.A. sites are more likely to display non-A.A. and/or controversial material. Linking might imply endorsement or affiliation, regardless of content. _(SMF-101 Q6)_

**Our local policy:**

- Prefer linking to known A.A. service entities and aa.org resources.
- Use a clear "you are leaving our site" exit notice for any external link (following GSO's practice on aa.org). _(MG-18; SMF-101 Q6)_
- All new outbound links must be reviewed by a designated human before going live.

**Source basis:** Tradition 6; Tradition 10; MG-18 Rev. 10/25; SMF-101 Q6

### 3.6 Copyright and A.A. Literature

**Default principle: Link, don't copy.** If material is currently available on aa.org or aagrapevine.org, link to the appropriate pages instead of uploading PDFs or reproducing content. _(MG-18; SMF-101 Q4)_

**Conditions:**

- Text must be presented as-is, with no amendments, changes, or editing.
- The proper credit line must be included.
- This does not extend to continuous or daily reprinting of excerpts. A.A.W.S. does not grant permission for anyone to continuously reprint excerpts through the Internet or electronic devices. _(aa.org Terms of Use — FAQ)_

### 3.7 Images, Graphics, and Protected Material

- Do not download, copy, or duplicate aa.org videos or graphic images without express written permission of A.A.W.S. _(aa.org footer notice)_
- Do not use The Blue People® graphic. It is reserved exclusively for the A.A.W.S. Board, AA Grapevine Board, the General Service Board, and GSO. A.A.W.S. does not grant permission to any other A.A. entity, nor to create derivatives. _(SMF-101 Q12; aa.org Terms of Use)_
- A.A.W.S. web banners (for linking to aa.org) may be used but must link to aa.org and may not be altered. A.A.W.S. reserves the right to have banners removed if the surrounding content is contrary to the Twelve Traditions. _(SMF-101 Q7)_

**Source basis:** aa.org Terms of Use; SMF-101 Q7, Q12

### 3.8 Speaker Recordings and Audio

- Members report that audio files of A.A. talks are increasingly disseminated over the Internet. If a member objects to having their A.A. story broadcast publicly, they may contact the site's administrator to request removal. _(MG-18)_
- Some A.A. members, if being recorded for public distribution, may choose to leave out identifying details about themselves or their families. _(MG-18)_
- Recordings posted publicly must observe anonymity at the public level — no full last names, no identifying details that link a specific person to A.A. membership.

**Source basis:** MG-18 Rev. 10/25; Tradition 11

### 3.9 Safety in Digital Spaces

- The principles that apply to in-person A.A. settings also apply to phone, online, and social media settings. _(SMF-209; MG-25; MG-18)_
- For safety purposes, provide helpful reminders that taking pictures/screenshots and posting on social media in a way that compromises A.A. members' privacy and consent are not in keeping with the anonymity principles of Traditions 11 and 12. _(MG-18, Rev. 10/25)_
- To help keep online meetings/platforms free from harassment, consider creating safety-related service positions. Online groups that have experienced harassment have adjusted security settings, created safety procedures, and/or sought professional technical support. _(MG-18; SMF-209; MG-25)_
- Anonymity is not a cloak protecting criminal or inappropriate behavior. _(SMF-209)_

**Source basis:** MG-18 Rev. 10/25; SMF-209; MG-25 Rev. 06/25; F-228

### 3.10 Nonaffiliation and Outside Entities

- A.A. is not affiliated with any treatment center, rehab, hospital, or any other organization or 12-Step Fellowship. _(MG-18)_
- No online A.A. or non-A.A. entity should purport itself to be a spokesperson for A.A. or act as if it represents the General Service Office, A.A.W.S., or the General Service Board. Each A.A. entity is autonomous and encouraged to make decisions by informed group conscience. _(MG-18)_
- Even A.A. meetings that take place at treatment facilities are not affiliated with those facilities. _(MG-18)_

**Source basis:** Tradition 6; Tradition 10; MG-18 Rev. 10/25

## 4 · HARD RULES — AI Agents

AI agents (LLMs, chatbots, automated content pipelines) **must follow ALL Human Hard Rules above**, plus these additional constraints:

### 4.1 No Fabrication (Zero Tolerance)

AI agents must **never** fabricate:

- Meeting times, locations, formats, or contact information.
- Committee contacts, phone numbers, or email addresses.
- Policies, procedures, or "official A.A. statements."
- Endorsements, recommendations, or claims about what A.A. "requires," "guarantees," or "certifies."
- Claims about A.A.'s effectiveness using superlative or promotional language.
- Advisory Actions, Conference decisions, or service material that the agent cannot verify.

**If information is not in the repository data, an allowed source, or verifiable context:** Respond with "This requires human confirmation" and halt content generation for that item.

### 4.2 No Sponsorship or Program Guidance

AI agents must **never**:

- Act as, or claim to be, an A.A. sponsor.
- Provide step work guidance, personal recovery advice, or therapeutic suggestions.
- Interpret A.A. literature for recovery purposes (e.g., "what the Big Book means for your situation").
- Replace or simulate the human connection that is central to A.A.'s program of recovery.

AI may factually describe what A.A. is, how meetings work, and where to find help — but must not simulate the spiritual or interpersonal dimensions of recovery.

**Rationale:** A.A.'s foundation is one alcoholic talking to another. AI cannot replicate this, and attempting to do so risks misleading vulnerable individuals and misrepresenting A.A.

### 4.3 Allowed Sources (Agent Allowlist)

AI agents may only cite or derive A.A. policy, guidance, and factual information from:

**Official A.A. sources:**

- aa.org (and its officially hosted documents, PDFs, and videos)
- aaws.widen.net PDFs linked from aa.org
- aagrapevine.org (for Grapevine/La Viña policy when needed)
- The A.A. Service Manual (current edition)

**Local meeting/event data must come from:**

- This repository's data files
- Approved intergroup/area sources (listed below)
- The Meeting Guide app API (if integrated)

**Approved local sources** (customize per project):

- `<LOCAL_INTERGROUP_SITE>`
- `<LOCAL_AREA_SITE>`
- `<MEETING_GUIDE_EXPORT_SOURCE>`

### 4.4 Red-Flag Filter (Must Halt and Escalate)

If a draft or generated output includes **any** of the following, the AI agent MUST stop and request human review before publishing:

- A person's full last name in an A.A. context
- A full-face photo reference, image tag, or image generation prompt for an identifiable person
- A "testimonial"-style recovery story that could identify a specific individual
- Superlative or promotional claims: "A.A. guarantees…", "best…", "#1…", "certified…", "proven cure…"
- Language implying endorsement, partnership, or sponsorship with outside entities
- Reproduction of A.A. literature beyond a brief excerpt (phrase/sentence/brief paragraph)
- Continuous or daily reprinting of A.A. literature excerpts
- Political, religious, or controversial-issue commentary tied to A.A. or presented as an A.A. position
- Content that could imply A.A. affiliation with treatment centers, rehabs, hospitals, or commercial services
- Medical advice, treatment recommendations, or clinical claims presented as A.A. guidance
- Content that could break another member's anonymity (even indirectly)

### 4.5 Output Rules

**Tone:** Informational, calm, welcoming, non-promotional. The voice should reflect attraction, not promotion.

**Preferred content patterns:**

- "What is A.A.?"
- "How to find a meeting" (with links to aa.org/find-aa and local resources)
- "What to expect at a meeting"
- "Contact information" (role-based emails, not personal addresses)
- "Anonymity and privacy reminders"
- "How A.A. is self-supporting"

**For A.A. literature references:** Always prefer linking to aa.org pages over copying text. When a brief excerpt is included, always include the proper credit line.

**Never generate:**

- Content implying A.A. has opinions on outside issues
- Comparisons ranking A.A. against other programs or treatments
- Content using A.A. trademarks in ways that could imply commercial affiliation
- Fictional A.A. member stories or composite narratives that blur the line between real and fabricated experience

### 4.6 AI Transparency

When AI-generated content is published publicly on an A.A.-related site, the site should disclose that AI tools were used in content creation where appropriate. This supports the principle of honesty that underlies A.A.'s Traditions and avoids any appearance of deception.

AI agents are not A.A. members. AI agents do not represent A.A. AI agents are tools used by A.A. members in service.

## 5 · SEO Guardrails

**Principle:** SEO is for **access** — helping people who need and want A.A. find us. It is not for marketing, competition, or self-promotion. _(Tradition 11; MG-18)_

### 5.1 Allowed

- Accurate page titles and meta descriptions ("Find an A.A. meeting in `<region>`", "What is A.A.?", "A.A. meeting schedule for `<area>`").
- Plain-language keywords that help people searching for help with a drinking problem.
- Structured data (schema.org) that describes meetings and events factually — time, format, location, accessibility notes.
- Linking to aa.org and local A.A. service entity sites.
- ADA-accessible website format. _(MG-18)_

### 5.2 Not Allowed (Local Policy Safeguards)

- Paid ads that claim official affiliation with A.A. or use A.A. registered marks in a misleading way.
- "Best," "top," "guaranteed," "#1," or other superlative/competitive language.
- Testimonials, reviews, or ratings about meetings.
- Claims about success rates, cure rates, or outcomes.
- Keywords or ad copy that could imply A.A. endorses or is affiliated with treatment centers, rehabs, or commercial services.
- SEO tactics that prioritize traffic over accuracy or that present A.A. in a promotional rather than informational light.

**Source basis:** Tradition 6; Tradition 10; Tradition 11; MG-18 Rev. 10/25

## 7 · Practical Enforcement (Repo-Level)

These are engineering controls — not A.A. policy, but how we enforce our policy in this codebase.

### 7.1 Required Human Review

The following must be reviewed by a designated human before going live:

- Any PR/merge that changes public-facing copy (homepage, SEO, meta descriptions).
- Any page that includes photos or images of people.
- Any new outbound link.
- Any content that quotes A.A. literature (verify brevity + credit line).
- Any AI-generated content intended for public publication.

### 7.2 Pre-Commit / CI Checks (Recommended)

Configure automated checks to flag (not necessarily block) commits that include:

- Common last-name patterns in member-context files (requires manual tuning).
- Image uploads without a `privacy-reviewed: true` flag in frontmatter/metadata.
- Large blocks of text that match known A.A. literature passages (copyright check).
- Missing credit lines adjacent to A.A. literature excerpts.
- Superlative language patterns: "best," "guaranteed," "#1," "top-rated," "certified," "proven."
- Email addresses in public-facing content (flag for BCC/privacy review).

### 7.3 AI Agent Configuration

- AI agents must be configured with this guardrails document as part of their system prompt or context.
- AI agents must have the red-flag filter (Section 4.4) implemented as a hard stop — not a soft warning.
- AI agent outputs destined for public pages must pass through human review before publication.
- AI agents must not be given write access to publish directly to production without a human-in-the-loop approval step.
- Logs of AI-generated content should be retained for audit and incident review.

### 7.4 Incident Response

**If anonymity is compromised:**

1. Immediately unpublish or remove the content.
2. Rotate any leaked contact information (email addresses, phone numbers).
3. Notify the site owner + the appropriate service committee (PI, web committee, etc.).
4. Document what happened and what changed to prevent recurrence.
5. If the breach involved AI-generated content, review the agent's configuration and add the failure pattern to the red-flag filter.

**If a copyright concern is raised:**

1. Remove the content immediately pending review.
2. Review against the brief-excerpt guidelines and credit-line requirements.
3. If in doubt, contact A.A.W.S. IP Policy: ippolicy@aa.org
4. Document the incident and update guardrails if needed.
